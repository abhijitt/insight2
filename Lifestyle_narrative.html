Narrative

The average lifestyle of an IITian is expected to be boring and dominated by academics.However when asked a few questions ranging from physical fitness to alcohol and nicotine the results were ‘as expected’ for anyone studying in IITB but might be shocking for those outside.

Physical Fitness:

  • 64.2% reported that participation in physical activities at least twice a week is necessary. 63% of the total male respondents chose this option while 82% female respondents selected this.

  • 19% were indifferent and 11.5% considered themselves to be fitness freaks.

Alcohol and nicotine:

  • Approximately 67% had consumed alcoholic beverages atleast once out of which 33% were mostly during treats. 20% consumed alcohol once a month while 14% once a week.

  • 69% men had consumed alcohol atleast once while 67% women had tried it.

  • 13% had started drinking before coming to IIT itself while 71% started after the completion of their freshmen year.

  • Sunny came up as the leading choice for drinking gathering 33% votes, followed closely by Laxmi at 30%. 14% preferred their rooms to be ideal spot for drinking.

  • The institute seemed to adhere to the ‘no smoking’ principle quite strongly as 72% had never touched a cigarette. 14% had a drag once every 2 or 3 weeks while 4% were chain smokers. 73% started smoking after their freshie year, 15% during the first year and 12% before the IIT chapter began

  • Out of the people who smoked, 95% had consumed alcohol while the vice versa was true for only 37%.

Monthly Expenditure:

  • Excluding academic and mess expenditure 31% students claimed they spent anywhere between 1000 and 3000 per month. 27% spent between 3000 and 5000 while expenses of 19% went above 5000. 8% pegged it below 1000 and 15% chose not to disclose it.

  • A stunning 68% claimed majority of their money spent was in food. 88% of this group either got used to the mess food at best or most of the times disliked mess food leading to inflated expenditure on food.The expenditure of only 41% of students who disliked mess food was below 3000 per month.

Bucket List:

  • 64.75% had visited the Maddu mess, which opens at 3:30 and closes at 8:00, and by the way that’s AM not PM. 62% completed the walk-in-the-park trek to Sameer Hill. Surprisingly, 49% have never been inside H10 while 16.2% have never visited the central library of the institute.

  • In the loving memory of ‘Dil Chahta Hain’, 52.4% went on a road trip to Goa while the iconic terrace scenes of ‘3 Idiots’ were (without alcohol, duh!) replicated by 39% who spent a night on the hostel terrace.

  • Almost 60% participated atleast once in the fierce competition that is the General Championship- An inter-hostel competition. Though the least selected option, 16.56% did visit the red light areas of Mumbai.
